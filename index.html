Wrath of the Bony Boys

Wrath of the Bony Boys
Wrath of the Bony Boys is a single-player, satire-horror game created over a five-week span. I worked with four other GDD students on this project - Simon Demirjian, Adam Hall, Drake Richards, and John Vance. This game has the player running around a foggy graveyard and forest to collect pumpkins, all while being chased by a troop of skeletons. These skeletons are floating, tumbling, and ragdolling toward the player, and every so often they jump into the air. I did the sound design for the game, the skeletons’ obstacle avoidance, and part of the menu system.

Two Gnomes in a Trenchcoat

Two Gnomes in a Trenchcoat
This game was made over the course of a semester in a research studio course focused on experimental gameplay. Two Gnomes in a Trenchcoat is a two-player cooperative audio-only game, meaning it has no visuals to speak of (hence the blank screenshot). The players take on the roles of two members of a tabletop RPG game group with an under-prepared and overly-spiteful game master (GM). The player characters are playing as two half-deaf gnomes in a trenchcoat. As such, the player on the left is given one side of a set of headphones, and the player on the right is given the other. The players hear independent audio. Both channels of audio are required to pass the quick-time events that make up the game, so communication is necessary.

Asteroids Replica

Asteroids Replica
This replica of the classic 80s game Asteroids was created in Unity 2D using C# scripts. I was the sole developer for this project. Just like the original game, this replica utilizes vector-based movement and forces to move objects around the screen. The goal is to destroy all of the asteroids on the screen by shooting them with the ship's laser. I themed the project around Star Fox, and as such, all of the sound clips in the game were taken from Fox McCloud's character in Super Smash Bros. Melee, and the ship itself is modeled after Fox's arwing ship from his main series.

Hectic Hockey

Hectic Hockey
Hectic Hockey is a two-player air hockey game where an additional puck spawns for each goal scored. This academic project was done using C++ in OpenGL. I handled collision resolution between pucks and goals, as well as most of the UI elements. Each object in the scene has a bounding sphere or box that can be visually toggled on or off. Our group used a grid for spatial partitioning, and I added a way to turn it on and off to showcase its effectiveness at keeping the frame rate up. Unfortunately, the frame rate only started tanking when several hundred pucks were on screen at once, so I also made the ‘P’ key add one-hundred pucks to the scene per press in order to get to that point.
